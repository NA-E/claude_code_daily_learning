**TOOL**

**The "How I AI Bench" Method — Build Your Own Model-Comparison Harness in ~45 Minutes**
*Source: Lenny's How I AI podcast (same digest)*

Claire built an HTML scoring page with Claude Code in about 45 minutes to hand-rate 64 outputs (1-to-5 gut score + notes, exported to JSON), then compared it against LLM-as-judge scoring from GPT-5.5 and Opus 4.8. The LLM judges clustered toward the middle and missed obvious failures (broken prototypes, ignored wireframe constraints) that she caught on a first screenshot glance; a 70/30 weighted index of her own score vs. the LLM judges flipped the ranking entirely, putting Sonnet 4.6 in first place instead of Gemini 3 Pro.

*Why it matters for you*: A concrete, buildable alternative to trusting vendor benchmarks or one-off vibe checks before adopting a new model for client work — and hard evidence that pure LLM-as-judge scoring undersells visible quality failures.

*Action*: Next time you're deciding whether to switch a client project's model, ask Claude Code to build you a lightweight HTML scoring page (prompt: "build me a single-page HTML tool that shows two outputs side by side, lets me score each 1-5, add a note, and export to JSON") before trusting a written review.

**FEATURE**

**Cloudflare Splits AI Bots Into Search / Agent / Training — New Domains Block Two of Three by Default Starting September 15, 2026**
*Source: The Neuron (theneuron@newsletter.theneurondaily.com, "Cloudflare draws an AI bot line") + corroborated by The AI Break*

Cloudflare rolled out AI traffic controls to all customers, including Free tier, splitting bot behavior into three categories: Search (crawl-and-send-traffic-back), Agent (act on a user's behalf), and Training (absorb content into a model). Starting September 15, 2026, new domains joining Cloudflare will block Agent and Training bots by default on ad-supported pages while Search stays allowed; multi-purpose crawlers combining Search and Training get blocked under the stricter rule if a site owner blocks Training.

*Why it matters for you*: Any client site you manage or launch on Cloudflare after September 15 inherits these defaults automatically — worth knowing before a client asks why their content stopped showing up in an AI agent's answers, or before you assume a new site is "AI-visible" by default.

*Action*: Use this exact audit prompt on your own or a client's site before September 15: "Audit my AI crawler policy for [URL]. Business model: [ads/subscriptions/lead gen/ecommerce/docs]. Create a crawler access plan covering what Search, Agent, and Training bots should each be allowed to do, which pages need stricter rules, and the risks of blocking too much vs. too little."
