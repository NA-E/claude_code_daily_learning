**A Claude Code Team Member Lays Out the Exact Fix This Routine Has Been Missing Since July 16**

*Source: The Curve / Peter Yang (peteryang+podcast@substack.com), "How I Plan, Build, and Run Loops with Claude Code in 40 Minutes," July 19, 1:05 PM UTC*

Thariq Shihipar, who works on the Claude Code team, walked Peter Yang through his actual loop-building playbook: use `/goal` only when you have a verifiable finish line (Yang tried "/goal Build me a great game" with no verifiable end state and got poor results); plan with Claude first specifically to surface unknowns before building, reading the plan manually rather than trusting it; use separate creator and verifier sub-agents rather than letting one agent grade its own work; and — most concretely — when a better model ships, the first move is to *remove* instructions from your system prompt and CLAUDE.md, not add to them, because hard rules and examples that worked around a weaker model's gaps now just constrain a stronger one. Anthropic built an interpretability tool called the Jacobian lens that surfaces a previously unseen internal layer in Claude, which the researchers call "J-space" — containing words related to what the model is likely to say in the near future, not just its immediate next token. *Action*: if you're building or evaluating a hallucination-detection approach, note that this is now the second interpretability lab (after Goodfire) independently reporting an internal signal that precedes a fabrication — worth tracking whether a third lab reports something similar before treating it as a reliable detection method.
