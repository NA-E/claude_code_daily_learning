**Late Interaction RAG: ColPali / PyLate / Mixedbread / Jina — Free Lesson June 10, 12PM PDT (Maven / Parlance Labs)**
Hamel Husain (Parlance Labs) and Isaac Flath are teaching a free 45-minute lesson on **late interaction RAG** on Wednesday June 10 at 12PM PDT. Core concept: instead of squashing each document into a single vector (standard single-vector embeddings), late interaction keeps information at the **token level** — each token gets its own representation and matching happens via **MaxSim** (maximum similarity across token pairs). Technique is called **PLAID quantization** for scalable deployment. Tools covered: Mixedbread, LightOn (PyLate), ColPali, Jina. Three single-vector failure modes this solves: (1) pooling loses fine-grained detail, (2) long context degrades retrieval, (3) out-of-domain queries fail. Late interaction works well out of the box with far less tuning.
*Source: Parlance Labs / Maven (parlance-labs@courses.maven.com), June 8, 2026*
**Why it matters for Nourin:** Any workflow you build that ingests long documents (meeting notes, client contracts, technical docs) and needs reliable retrieval will hit the three single-vector failure modes. The 2026-06-08 Hermes Agent entry tracked persistent cross-session context architecture; late interaction RAG solves the *retrieval* layer underneath that. If you're building a RAG system for any client or personal workflow, this is the current state-of-the-art retrieval technique.
**Action:** Register for the free lesson at the link in the Maven email (Wednesday June 10, 12PM PDT). Even if you miss it live, sign up to get the recording. This is Hamel Husain — one of the sharpest evals/RAG practitioners in the space.
