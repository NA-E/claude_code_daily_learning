### **TOOL**

**ElevenLabs v3 Audio Tags — [excited] [whispers] [sighs] Embedded in Scripts for Vocal Control (The Neuron)**
ElevenLabs v3 introduced **Audio Tags**: inline direction cues embedded directly in scripts to control vocal performance. Format: bracket tags placed in the text itself, e.g., `[happily][shouts] We did it! [laughs] I can't believe it.` vs the flat version: `We did it. I can't believe it.` Supported tags include: `[excited]`, `[whispers]`, `[sighs]`, `[shouts]`, `[laughs]`, `[happily]`, and more. ElevenLabs calls this "an essential skill with v3" — the previous approach (long voice descriptions in the prompt) is replaced by in-context direction. The tradeoff: requires more prompt engineering upfront, but produces far more expressive, controllable outputs. V3 is available in the ElevenLabs dashboard at current pricing tiers.
Source: The Neuron (theneuron@newsletter.theneurondaily.com, May 26).
**Why it matters for you:** If you use ElevenLabs for any audio content (podcast-style digests, voiceovers, client-facing audio), v3 Audio Tags change how you write your scripts. The voice-over layer of the Synthesia workflow above (scene scripts with AI avatars) is where Audio Tags apply most directly — you can now write emotion direction directly into the scene scripts.
**Action:** On your next ElevenLabs generation, test one sentence with Audio Tags vs without. Example: `[warmly][pauses] Here's what this means for you.` vs `Here's what this means for you.` The delta in expressiveness takes 30 seconds to test and immediately tells you whether to migrate your script-writing process to the tagged format.
