**CMU/Stanford Agent Benchmark Study — Current Benchmarks Cover Less Than 50% of Real Work; Management ($1.3T in Wages) Nearly Unmeasured (The Batch, DeepLearning.ai, May 22)**
Researchers at Carnegie Mellon and Stanford analyzed 10,000+ examples from 43 agent benchmarks (SWE-bench, WebArena, and 41 others) mapped to U.S. labor statistics via O*NET. Key finding: agent benchmarks heavily over-represent software engineering (8,622 examples) versus reality (5.2M U.S. workers in "computer and mathematical" occupations). Management occupations — $1,326.3B in annual wages, 11M workers — are barely covered. The best single benchmark (GDPval) covered only 47.8% of work activities and 58.5% of skill categories. All 43 benchmarks combined: 56.5% of work activities, 85.4% of skill categories. Implication: agents built to pass current benchmarks are optimized for a narrow slice of economic value. The highest-leverage agent categories for real-world impact are administrative, financial, and managerial.

Action: When evaluating whether to build an agent for a task, ask: "Is this task in a benchmark?" If yes, there's already competition. If no, you may be in whitespace. Use this framework to prioritize your next automation project.
