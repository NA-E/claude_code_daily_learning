### **CONCEPT**

**Leitwörter ("Leading Words") — Hardwiring Agent Behavior via Repeated Key Phrases**
From The Neuron, based on Matt Pocock's `/teach` skill (github.com/mattpocock/skills): pick **one phrase** that carries the entire behavior you want (e.g., "zone of proximal development"). Repeat it 2-3 times in your skill or prompt. The model begins using the phrase in its own reasoning/thinking traces to guide behavior — it becomes self-referential. Template:
```
Use [LEITWORT] as your operating principle for this task.
By [LEITWORT], I mean: [simple definition of the behavior you want].
Apply that principle while you work. Before giving the final answer,
check whether the output follows [LEITWORT] and revise once if needed.
Task: [task] | Context: [context] | Output format: [format]
```
Good example leitwörter: "precision over coverage," "show don't tell," "actionable over informational."
- **Source:** The Neuron (theneuron@newsletter.theneurondaily.com), via Matt Pocock
- **Why it matters for you:** CLAUDE.md is a natural home for 1-2 leitwörter that define your output standards across all sessions — this solves the quality consistency problem.
- **Action:** Pick one leitwort for your CLAUDE.md that encapsulates your ideal output behavior. Write it 3 times across the file in different contexts.
