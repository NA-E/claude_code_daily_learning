**CONCEPT**

**Anthropic Finds "J-Space" — an Internal Workspace Where Claude Holds Concepts Before They Reach the Answer**
*Source: The Neuron (theneuron@newsletter.theneurondaily.com, "Anthropic found Claude's hidden workspace") + The Rundown AI (news@daily.therundown.ai, "The part of Claude's brain nobody built"), both dated July 7*

Anthropic's new research ([anthropic.com/research/global-workspace](https://www.anthropic.com/research/global-workspace), [full paper](https://transformer-circuits.pub/2026/workspace/index.html)) identifies "J-space" — a small set of internal neural signals that function like a shared mental whiteboard, holding and routing concepts before they surface in text. In one test, Claude was asked "how many legs does the animal that spins webs have?"; it internally loaded "spider" and answered 8. When researchers swapped the internal concept to "ant," the answer flipped to 6 — without the prompt changing. When researchers suppressed J-space entirely, Claude kept writing fluent text but its multi-step reasoning collapsed. Both newsletters independently ran this as their lead story the same day, and The Rundown notes Anthropic explicitly declined to claim this proves anything about consciousness, in response to public criticism from Microsoft AI head Mustafa Suleyman.

*Why it matters for you*: This is a different kind of Anthropic story than the pricing/access churn this repo has tracked since May — it's the first entry pointing at mechanistic interpretability (reading a model's internal state, not just its output) as a concrete research direction, which is also the technical backbone of why "prompt injection detection" and "hidden goal detection" are becoming realistic safety features rather than hopes.

*Action*: No build action needed today — file as a "why Claude behaves the way it does" reference. If you want the primary source, read the actual research post at anthropic.com/research/global-workspace before repeating any secondhand interpretation of it to a client.
