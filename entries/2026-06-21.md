### **CONCEPT**

**Loops: Agents That Review Their Own Output in Rounds Until Good Enough**
From Every.to's piece: "Loops" is a workflow pattern where you give an agent a goal, and it works in rounds — generating output, reviewing it against the goal, re-prompting itself, and iterating until the output clears a quality threshold. Every.to's head of growth Austin Tedesco used this pattern to build an overnight NBA simulator and to rework Every's entire subscription flow. The key distinction from a simple retry: the agent's review step is itself a prompt — the agent is asked to evaluate its own output against explicit criteria before deciding whether to continue or stop.
- **Source:** Every.to (hello@every.to) — "Built on Moving Ground" + "Loops for Non-Coders," June 21, 2026
- **Why it matters for Nourin:** The June 17 entry named "session amnesia" and the May 12 entry tracked "Routines + Dreaming ship." Loops is the next pattern up the ladder — it's not about what persists between sessions (CLAUDE.md), it's about quality control within a single session. If you run any multi-step task in Claude Code, adding a loop review step is likely the highest-ROI structural change you can make right now.
- **Action:** Pick one multi-step Claude Code task you run regularly. Add a review step at the end: *"Now review your output against these criteria: [list]. If it fails any, revise once and stop."* Run it 3 times and note whether quality improves without re-prompting.

**The "Middle 80% Principle": Point Claude at Your Bottleneck, Not Your Whole Job**
Artificial Corner (Mario Estrella) published a framework for deploying Claude in a business: every workflow has a chain (Reach → Leads → Sales → Delivery → Retention); the middle 80% of each stage is where Claude operates best — the parts that are neither the hardest 10% (which require judgment) nor the easiest 10% (which don't need AI). The correct first question is not "what can I automate" but "if I 10x this task tomorrow, would my revenue actually go up?" The answer locates your bottleneck; that's where Claude goes first. Estrella applied this to his own AI newsletter: his bottleneck was reach, not content generation, so he automated republishing and social adaption — not article writing — and grew subscriber numbers.
- **Source:** Artificial Corner (artificialcorner@substack.com) — "How to Use Claude In Your Business," June 21, 2026
- **Why it matters for Nourin:** The June 17 entry's "34 agents" framing (AI with Allie newsletter) gave you the org-chart lens. This gives you the diagnostic tool to figure out where in that org chart to deploy Claude first. It's the operational counterpart to the "Claude Code feature ladder" from June 20.
- **Action:** Write out your workflow chain (adjust for your context). For each stage, ask: "if I 10x this, does revenue/output go up?" Find the first "yes" — that's your first Claude deployment target. Build there, not where AI looks impressive.
