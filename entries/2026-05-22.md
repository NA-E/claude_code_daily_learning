### **WORKFLOW**

**Claude 5-Lane Routing Framework — Where to Put Each Workflow Before June 15**
CoworkOperator (coworkoperator.com) published the most precise architectural map of Claude's product surfaces yet. Five distinct lanes — each with a different review point, budget owner, and risk profile: **(1) Claude Chat** — questions, drafts, summaries, human present; **(2) Claude Cowork** — context-heavy multi-step business deliverables with human approval gate; **(3) Interactive Claude Code** — coding while a person actively steers in terminal/IDE; **(4) Agent SDK / `claude -p`** — programmatic Claude calls from scripts, apps, GitHub Actions, third-party tools; **(5) Managed Agents** — long-running sessions needing state, tools, files, private network access, controlled runtime. The routing decision rule: "Put the work where the review point, budget owner, and risk level match." A GitHub Action that posts output to Slack is Lane 4, not Lane 3 — even though both involve code.
**Source:** CoworkOperator Substack (claudedesktop@substack.com), "Claude just exposed the workflow nobody owns," May 22, 2026.
**Why it matters for you:** If any workflow you built for yourself is now part of a team's rhythm — touching Slack, GitHub, customer data, or billing — it doesn't belong in your personal credit bucket anymore. That mismatch becomes a budget and reliability problem on June 15.
**Action:** Write down every workflow where Claude currently affects real work. Tag each with one of the 5 lane labels. Flag anything tagged Lane 4 or 5 that currently runs on your personal subscription. That's the list you need to act on before June 15.
