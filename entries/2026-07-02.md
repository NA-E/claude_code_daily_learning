**6 Rules for Prompting Fable 5, Translated From Anthropic's Own Guide**
*Source: Alex McFarland Substack ("Fable 5 is back. You have until July 7 to get the most out of it.")*

McFarland read Anthropic's official "Prompting Claude Fable 5" doc (platform.claude.com/docs, written for developers) and translated it for operators. Six rules that survive translation: **(1) give it the reason, not just the request** — context lets Fable 5 connect the task to the right files instead of guessing intent; **(2) tell it to act** — the exact line from Anthropic's guide is *"When you have enough information to act, act,"* aimed at Fable 5's tendency to overplan and re-research on ambiguous asks; **(3) make it prove progress** — Anthropic reported this nearly eliminated fabricated status reports even on tasks designed to bait them, with the instruction *"Before you report progress, check every claim against an actual result... If something isn't verified yet, say so explicitly"*; **(4) separate looking from doing** — on thinking-out-loud prompts, the deliverable should be an assessment, not an unrequested fix (Anthropic's own example: Fable 5 drafting an email nobody asked for); **(5) say less** — Anthropic states that prompt scaffolding tuned for older models (15 numbered rules, step-by-step choreography) can *degrade* Fable 5's output, since one plain sentence now does what a page of rules used to; **(7) give it a place to write things down** — a plain markdown lessons file, one lesson per note, updated not duplicated, checked at the start of every session.

*Why it matters for you*: Rules 2–4 are meant to live permanently in CLAUDE.md rather than being retyped per session; rule 5 means some of your existing prompt scaffolding may now be actively counterproductive on Fable 5.

*Action*: Add rules 2, 3, and 4 to this repo's CLAUDE.md tonight, verbatim as quoted above, so every future Fable 5 session in this workspace starts with them pre-installed.

**CONCEPT**

**Rule 6: Never Ask Fable 5 to "Show Its Reasoning" — It Can Trigger a Silent Downgrade**
*Source: Alex McFarland Substack (same piece)*

Fable 5's safety classifiers screen for attempts to extract the model's internal reasoning. Any saved prompt or skill that says "explain your reasoning" or "show your thinking" can be read as exactly that kind of extraction attempt and get declined — and on the **Claude apps specifically**, a declined request is quietly picked up by **Opus 4.8 instead**, with no notification that you got the second-best model instead of the one you asked for.

*Why it matters for you*: This is a silent, undetectable failure mode distinct from the sanctioned fallback described above — you'd have no way to know it happened unless you already suspected it.

*Action*: Tonight, grep every saved Claude Code skill, prompt template, and CLAUDE.md file in your setup for "explain your reasoning" or "show your thinking" and delete or rephrase those lines before your next Fable 5 session.
